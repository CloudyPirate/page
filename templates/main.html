Alleyrat Esports Club

Here is how the alleyrats came to be.

Counter-Strike Club

For many, clubs, sports/esports, and competition are only available to them in scholastic environments. Outside of these educational settings, there is little opportunity to participate in these passions in an organized manner, with the common goal of team-competition. Additionally, many of these activities possess barriers to entry that prevent new players from even attempting to join these communities. For Counter-Strike specifically, the current system requires new players, regardless of how talented they may be, to clog through at least 4 tiers of organized play to even be considered as a “competitive player.” This barrier to entry is compounded by the fact that many competitively-oriented, veteran players do not want to engage with “subpar teamplay” and have little interest in orienting new players to the game.

Currently, the only metric that evaluates a players competitive impact is HLTV rating, which is confusing when the game is not in an organized environment. This ultimately results in our competitive scene being divided by matchmaking systems (and platforms) that do not guarantee fair matches for new players to learn, nor present good ways for veteran players to actually play the game in a professional manner. Having a place for organized recreational competitive leagues helps solve the barrier to entry problem by giving community members the space to enjoy their hobby on the terms that they want, whether that means casually learning the game with their friends, or “try-harding” their way through the competitive brackets.

Our mission is to organize competitive Counter-Strike games so that every member of the community can participate regardless of time-commitment or skill. Not only is this about organizing meaningful Counter-Strike games, this is about creating a space where true competition is part of the fun. To achieve this goal, The Alley Rats Esports Club aims to offer a recreational league that can act as a stepping-stone for new competitive players while offering travel and team coaching opportunities to build up the broader Midwest CS community.

Competitive Travel Teams

With numerous local leagues, online leagues at the highschool, collegiate and amatuer levels, many players do not know where they can start their competitive journey.

We believe that playing in a league where most of the competitive playerbase competes on is crucial for player development and scouting.

Weekly Recreational Leagues

Not everyone has the time or the commitment to play games for more than a couple of hours a week; If this is more your tone, we offer recreational leagues as stepping stones into the competitive scene!
